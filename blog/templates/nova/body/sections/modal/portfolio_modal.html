Science Workshops

Conducted at youth centres and schools, focussing on young people aged between 7-10. Each workshop centres around a particular science theme, e.g ‘When will the sun burn out?’ . The hacking education mentor gives a short introduction, explaining the fundamental science related to the theme. During the introduction, the young people are encouraged to ask questions and are posed questions. The young people then use computers to independently learn and research the answer to their own questions, with guidance from the undergraduate. The aim of these science workshops is to spark scientific curiosity and cultivate the young person’s questioning mind in an informal learning environment.

Magazine

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

Following our science workshops, we are using the content to create a science magazine. The magazine will be a collection of science posters, alongside illustrations created by the young people, to produce a public magazine. Each poster contains information on a science topic, its current areas of research, and poses further questions for the reader to explore. The posters act as self contained science lessons, helping to facilitate independent learning. The magazine will be distributed to schools and families, and will be used for science workshops at youth centers and libraries. The first edition of the magazine is expected to be in print and ready for distribution by November 2018.
