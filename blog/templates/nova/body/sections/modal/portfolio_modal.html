Science Workshops

Informal Science

Conducted at youth centres and schools, focusing on young people aged between 7-10. Each workshop centres around a particular science theme, e.g ‘When will the sun burn out?’ . The hacking education mentor gives a short introduction, explaining the fundamental science related to the theme. During the introduction, the young people are encouraged to ask questions and are posed questions. The young people then use computers to independently learn and research the answer to their own questions, with guidance from the undergraduate. The aim of these science workshops is to spark scientific curiosity and cultivate the young person’s questioning mind in an informal learning environment.

Programming Projects

Intersection of science, technology and creativity.

Project based learning conducted in schools, for young people aged between 13-16. Taking place over a 12 week period, young people start off with practical coding exercises and then decide on a project, where they create something of their choice. hackingEducation mentors provide assistance and guidance when needed. E.g One group is creating a revision tool using a Raspberry Pi and the Google Assistant API, giving them practical experience with machine learning and Unix based systems. Young people are then given the opportunity to expand on their project, in an academic setting, during a 3 day workshop hosted at UCL. The aim of the programming projects is to give young people freedom to choose what they learn, and to cultivate the skills at the intersection of science, technology and creativity.

Map To Breathe

Issues & Solutions

Health inequity between people of different class, race and gender is a direct result of the effects of low air quality. Humans on average spend 70% of their time indoors, with that number being on the higher side for women especially women of colour. In the mapToBreathe project we’re developing low-cost high accuracy air quality measuring devices to map air quality, specifically indoor air quality in areas of low ventilation. We’re going to find the pollutant hotspots and place specific plants in those hotspots that could reduce the concentration of these pollutants. The project is going to be completed by a group of 8 sixth form students under the supervision of PhD students. A mapToBreathe will contribute to active research in improving air quality, but we also seek to create agents of change who will further the fight to protect the environment. Please visit www.mapToBreathe.org to find out more

A History of Race & Resistance in Britain

Lecture Series & festival of culture

hackingEducation is in the process of planning a history of race project in collaboration with Consented. "A History of Race & Resistance in Britain" aims to create a free lecture series on black history in Britain. A curriculum has been devised, and will be delivered by renowned speakers in the field. The history of Race lecture series will take place over 2 days, on the 9th and 10th of August (see our events), where they will be recorded to create a public online learning resource.The lectures will be interlaced with workshops and performances, producing a festival of culture. As well as being recorded, the event will be open to school children as well as the general public. Register for the event here!

Magazine

Young People & Parents

Following our science workshops, we are using the content to create a science magazine. The magazine will be a collection of science posters, alongside illustrations created by the young people, to produce a public magazine. Each poster contains information on a science topic, its current areas of research, and poses further questions for the reader to explore. The posters act as self contained science lessons, helping to facilitate independent learning. The magazine will be distributed to schools and families, and will be used for science workshops at youth centers and libraries. The first edition of the magazine is expected to be in print and ready for distribution by November 2018.
